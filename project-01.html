Project Context

The platform and where i came in.

A government agency sought clear visualizations for a centralized digital platform designed to track sustainability projects across the Philippines and support data-driven initiatives. As the main UI designer, I collaborated closely with a design colleague to establish the platform’s visual direction and deliver responsive, WCAG-compliant mockups covering over 20 distinct use cases.

I also bridged design and development by translating mockups into production-ready code using HTML, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript. I worked closely with developers and data engineers to ensure visual consistency and supported stakeholder presentations by walking through design progress and interaction flows.

**Some text content and visual elements, including logos, application name, design layouts and color palettes, have been modified to comply with NDA restrictions.

Problem

Complex needs,
No visual language.

While the project had a complete set of use cases, information architecture, and sitemap, the project lacked a cohesive visual direction and user interface that could support the platform’s complexity.

Goals

Designing with intent

At its core: build an accessible, user-friendly platform that enables stakeholders to easily explore and understand key development initiatives.

What this means:
Design simple, inclusive, and responsive interfaces for both desktop and mobile views.

Working on the frontend, adherence to semantic HTML principles and accessible markup is essential to meet WCAG guidelines.

Branding Process

Visual identity approach

Due to NDA restrictions, the original visual assets cannot be publicly shared. The visuals presented here have been sanitized to reflect the design intent without disclosing sensitive materials.

The color palette was based on a well-known public framework, chosen to align with the platform’s mission of supporting community-centered initiatives. Roboto was selected for its clean, legible appearance, while Roboto Mono was used to enhance the clarity of numerical data. While the main logo was provided, the rest of the interface was crafted to ensure a consistent, accessible, and user-friendly visual system.

Design Solutions

1. Simple, Clean layout

  • Easy to scan and understand.

Given the fast-paced timeline, the team prioritized high-fidelity mockups over low-fidelity wireframes, focusing on visualizing established use cases and refining designs through feedback.

2. Inclusive design

  • The actual layout meets WCAG color contrast requirements with a ratio of 4.5:1, achieving AA compliance for text.

3. Smooth interactions

  • What was once a complex use case is now presented through a simplified visual layout and intuitive interaction flow, making it easy to navigate.
